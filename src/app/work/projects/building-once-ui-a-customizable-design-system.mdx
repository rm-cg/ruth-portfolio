## Project Overview
Due to geographical and climatic conditions, typhoons often hit the Philippines, leading to massive flooding. This quantitative experimental study was conducted to investigate the feasibility of using a sensor-based automated sewerage system (S.B.A.S.S.) to manage and reduce floodwater effectively.

## My Role & Contributions
- **Experimental Design:** Employed quantitative and quasi-experimental research designs to test the system's effectiveness across varying conditions.
- **Technical Prototyping:** Assisted in the physical construction and technical setup of the sensor-based automated system.
- **Data Analysis:** Applied statistical analysis, including One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA), to differentiate and measure floodwater levels based on temperature and time exposure.

## Key Results
Through rigorous observation and data gathering across multiple setups, the findings confirmed a significant difference (p < 0.05) in reducing floodwater levels. The research successfully demonstrated that automation is a highly feasible and efficient mechanism for floodwater flow management.
