## Overview

Due to geographical and climatic conditions, typhoons often hit the Philippines, leading to massive flooding [1]. This quantitative experimental study was conducted to investigate the feasibility of using a sensor-based automated sewerage system (S.B.A.S.S.) to manage and reduce floodwater effectively [1].

## Key Features

- **Experimental Design**: Employed quantitative and quasi-experimental research designs to test the effectiveness of a sensor-based flood reduction system [2].
- **Technical Prototyping**: Assisted in the physical construction and experimental setup of the sensor-based automated system to validate efficiency [1, 3].
- **Statistical Analysis**: Applied One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) and tabular comparisons to differentiate floodwater levels across varying conditions like temperature and time [4, 5].

## Technologies Used

- **ANOVA Testing**: Used for rigorous statistical analysis of the experimental data [4].
- **Physical Prototyping**: Constructed a sensor-based system to simulate automated floodwater flow management [4].
- **Data Visualization**: Designed visual materials such as system diagrams and flood level data charts [6].

## Role and Contributions

I conducted the experimental design and testing, gathering and analyzing data across different setups [5]. I also presented the key findings to students, faculty, and local officials at the PANUKIDUKI Research Fair, addressing questions on statistical results and real-world applications [6, 7].

## Outcome

The findings confirmed the system's effectiveness, showing a significant difference (p < 0.05) in reducing floodwater levels and demonstrating the feasibility of automation for flood management [4, 5]. The project was heavily recognized, earning the Best in Research Prototype Award, Teacher's Choice Award, and Best in Audio-Visual Presentation at the PANUKIDUKI Research Fair [7].
