## Overview

This descriptive-correlation study was conducted at Paranaque National High School–Main to investigate the relationship between students’ socioeconomic status and their academic performance during the Covid-19 pandemic distance learning setup.

## Key Findings & Data Analysis

- **Correlation Results**: Found a significant relationship between socioeconomic status and students’ general averages during distance learning.
- **Influencing Factors**: Identified key indicators affecting performance, primarily parents’ educational attainment, occupation, total income, and family size.
- **Digital Inequality**: The data gathered provided strong evidence of digital inequality’s role in academic performance, proving that socioeconomic privileges directly correlated with uncompromised education during pandemic conditions.
