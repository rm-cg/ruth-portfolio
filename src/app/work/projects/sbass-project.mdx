## Overview

Due to geographical and climatic conditions, typhoons often hit the Philippines, leading to massive flooding. This quantitative experimental study was conducted to investigate the feasibility of using a sensor-based automated sewerage system (S.B.A.S.S.) to manage and reduce floodwater effectively.

## Key Features

- **Experimental Design**: Employed quantitative and quasi-experimental research designs to test the effectiveness of a sensor-based flood reduction system.
- **Technical Prototyping**: Assisted in the physical construction and experimental setup of the sensor-based automated system to validate efficiency.
- **Statistical Analysis**: Applied One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) and tabular comparisons to differentiate floodwater levels across varying conditions like temperature and time.

## Technologies Used

- **ANOVA Testing**: Used for rigorous statistical analysis of the experimental data.
- **Physical Prototyping**: Constructed a sensor-based system to simulate automated floodwater flow management.
- **Data Visualization**: Designed visual materials such as system diagrams and flood level data charts.

## Role and Contributions

I conducted the experimental design and testing, gathering and analyzing data across different setups. I also presented the key findings to students, faculty, and local officials at the PANUKIDUKI Research Fair, addressing questions on statistical results and real-world applications.

## Outcome

The findings confirmed the system's effectiveness, showing a significant difference (p < 0.05) in reducing floodwater levels and demonstrating the feasibility of automation for flood management. The project was heavily recognized, earning the Best in Research Prototype Award, Teacher's Choice Award, and Best in Audio-Visual Presentation at the PANUKIDUKI Research Fair.
