## The Intersection of Numbers and Real Life

As a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics student minoring in Computer Science at Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila (PLM), my academic life has always been rooted in equations, abstract theorems, and logical reasoning. However, my decision to pivot specifically into Data Analytics stemmed from a much more grounded and practical place: I simply love Excel. 

Long before I understood what a "Data Analyst" formally did, I found an inexplicable joy in looking at graphs, organizing chaotic information, and structuring data in my everyday life. Whether it was planning out schedules or categorizing files, I have always been deeply satisfied by the process of turning a mess into a clean, readable format. 

I quickly realized that while Computer Science students have a specialized scope in software architecture and development, being a Mathematics major gives me a remarkably unique vantage point. Data Science is not just about writing code, it is about understanding the math beneath the datasets. My background provides me with the flexibility to approach problems differently, applying analytical skills and advanced pattern recognition to extract stories from raw numbers. Math majors possess the versatility to branch into highly diverse fields, and for me, Data Analytics is the perfect intersection of logical theory, programming, and real-world organization.

## Leadership in Disguise: Data Management at GDGoC

When people think of organizational leadership or secretarial work, they often picture purely administrative or clerical tasks. However, in my experience, leadership is heavily reliant on data handling and organizational efficiency. 

In my role as the Lead Secretariat for the Google Developer Groups on Campus (GDGoC) - PLM, and previously as an officer for the PLM Mathematical Society, I essentially function as a data manager besides our separate organization's data committee. Whenever we launch an initiative, I am the one handling the influx of event registrants, analyzing post-event evaluation forms, tracking attendance metrics, and profiling the diverse backgrounds and interests of our participants. 

Furthermore, my integration with other committees requires me to compile and analyze all of this operational data to create comprehensive accomplishment reports. I genuinely enjoy building structured trackers, cleaning messy survey responses, and translating that information into actionable insights for the organization. Without realizing it, I have been practicing the meticulous data-cleaning and profiling skills required of a professional Data Analyst, just within a community-leadership setting.

## Research with a Purpose: Social Impact Analytics

My passion for data goes beyond corporate dashboards and organizational trackers; it is deeply rooted in Social Impact Analytics. I believe that data should be used to create solutions that genuinely help people. 

This mindset was solidified during my time as the Lead Researcher for the S.B.A.S.S. (Sensor-based Automated Sewerage System) project back in high school. We employed quantitative and quasi-experimental research designs to test a flood management prototype. By applying statistical analysis to differentiate floodwater levels across varying environmental conditions, we were able to prove the system's effectiveness in mitigating flood risks. Earning the *Teacher’s Choice* and *Best in Research Prototype* awards for that study taught me a valuable lesson: when you combine logic with the desire to help your community, the impact is profound.

## Looking Ahead

Right now, my ultimate career goal is to explore. I believe that an aspiring Data Analyst must be gradual and progressive, embracing continuous learning rather than aggressively rushing the process. True mastery takes time, patience, and the right environment.

Because of this progressive mindset, there is one specific milestone I am actively eyeing: securing an internship with Eskwelabs. I have deeply researched their programs and I am incredibly drawn to the quality of their institution. I admire how structured and prepared their organization is, the high caliber of their mentors, and their dedication to providing free learning sessions for those eager to upskill. Beyond their technical excellence, I appreciate their leniency and flexibility, proving that they truly understand and support student needs. 

Getting the opportunity to learn from a real team, handle real-world data projects, and collaborate with like-minded professionals would be a massive, official step forward in my career. Until then, I will continue expanding my analytical toolkit, staying ready for when that door finally opens.
